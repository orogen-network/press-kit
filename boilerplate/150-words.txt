Orogen is an open network design for verifiable LLM inference. Forge is the live testnet exercising an OpenAI-compatible gateway, GPU operators whose hardware is attested by multi-vendor TEE (Intel TDX, AMD SEV-SNP, NVIDIA Confidential Computing), validator replay, on-chain receipts, opML challenge windows, and zkML proofs for residual cases.

Settlement is designed around a pallet-enforced burn-and-mint engine on a Substrate-based chain with Frontier EVM compatibility and GRANDPA finality. Customers pay in non-transferable Compute Unit Credits (CUC), which the gateway burns; operator settlement is indexed to a USD-denominated rate that tracks real burn. Emission is demand-elastic with a 5%/year supply ceiling and a rolling-180-day burn-cap floor.

The network token is OROG. The stable compute credit is CUC. Forge readiness is tracked through the published release gates; mainnet TGE follows audit completion. Hardware spans six tiers from datacenter-premium to embed-only, each with its own stake floor and SLA. Documentation, RFCs, and SDKs live at orogen.network.
