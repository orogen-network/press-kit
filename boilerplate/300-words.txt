Orogen is a decentralized network design for verifiable LLM inference. Forge is being prepared to exercise an OpenAI-compatible gateway, GPU operators whose hardware is attested by multi-vendor TEE (Intel TDX, AMD SEV-SNP, NVIDIA Confidential Computing), validator replay over identical hardware, on-chain receipts, opML challenge windows, and zkML proofs for residual cases. Eight verification layers compound rather than rely on any single guarantee.

Settlement is designed around a pallet-enforced burn-and-mint engine on a Substrate-based chain (Polkadot SDK, Frontier EVM JSON-RPC, GRANDPA finality, ~12-second blocks). Customers pay in non-transferable Compute Unit Credits (CUC) with a 30-day expiry; the gateway burns CUC at job completion and operator settlement tracks real burn at a USD-denominated rate. The emission schedule is demand-elastic with a 5%/year supply ceiling and a rolling-90-day burn-cap floor — no foundation mint discretion.

Operators self-declare into one of six hardware tiers from datacenter-premium (Hopper/Blackwell with full attestation) down to embed-only (CPU edge), each with its own stake floor and SLA. Operator and validator settlement is tied to completed inference, replay, and anomaly detection. Customers get drop-in OpenAI compatibility: existing application code works against a different base URL.

The network token is OROG, with a 75/15/5/5 emission split across operators, validators, treasury, and ecosystem. Locked-backer and team allocations follow long vesting schedules. Governance is on-chain after mainnet TGE; foundation control of the mint is contractually capped, not policy-capped.

Forge testnet readiness is tracked through the published release gates. The release gates cover gateway routing, operator registration, validator replay, on-chain receipts, public smoke, and release evidence. Mainnet TGE follows multi-firm audit completion. Documentation, RFCs, downloads, and the source code live at orogen.network.
